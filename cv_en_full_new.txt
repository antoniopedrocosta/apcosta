Coordinator of "webQDA - Software to Support Qualitative Data Analysis". webQDA is a software to support the analysis of qualitative data in a collaborative and distributed environment. Although there are software packages that process non-numerical and unstructured data (text, image, video, audio) in qualitative analysis, few can be used by several researchers, in a collaborative and distributed work environment as the Internet can offer. In an academic context, webQDA is especially useful for researchers, master's, doctoral and postgraduate students who develop qualitative data analysis from any computer with Internet access. In a business context, webQDA has application in the analysis of data from the market, such as consumer and employee feedback, social media comments and competitor information to increase productivity, save time and gain competitive advantages. The project had two phases, the first with great involvement of the company Esfera Crítica (until 2015), the second with great involvement of Micro I/O (since 2015).
